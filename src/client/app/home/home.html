What is NatureNet?

A community of citizen environmentalists whose members keep making it better.

NatureNet combines citizen science and crowdsourced design. Citizen science is when ordinary people help collect and analyze data for real scientific research. Crowdsourced design is when ordinary people help improve a product through ideas, critique and feedback. We’ve created an online community where people can do both -- help environmentalists at sites around the country study and preserve your local area, as well as be part of making the technology they use to do it better for everyone. NatureNet is funded by the National Science Foundation (IIS-1221513) in a grant to the University of North Carolina at Charlotte, the University of Maryland, and the University of Colorado. The project is in coordination with the Anacostia Watershed Society (link), the Reedy Creek Nature Preserve (link) and the Aspen Centre for Environmental Studies.

Why join NatureNet?

Do you want to participate in a biodiversity-preserving project in your area? Or do you have an idea for a new project and want to get a community behind you? Join NatureNet today, and don’t just be a voice in the crowd: be part of a crowd with a voice!
